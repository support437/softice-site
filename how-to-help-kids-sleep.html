How to Help Kids Sleep

Updated March 2026 – New sleep tips added weekly.

Helping kids sleep better is one of the most common challenges for parents. If your child struggles at bedtime, small changes in routine can make a big difference.

With simple, consistent habits, bedtime can become calm, predictable, and much easier every night.

Create a Consistent Bedtime Routine

Children thrive on consistency. Going to bed at the same time every night helps regulate their internal clock.

A consistent bedtime routine helps kids fall asleep faster and improves overall sleep quality.

A simple routine can include:

  • Bath time or quiet play
  • Brushing teeth
  • A calming bedtime story
  • Lights out at the same time each night

Reduce Stimulation Before Bed

Screens and high-energy activities can make it harder for children to relax. Try to reduce stimulation at least 30–60 minutes before bedtime.

Use Calming Bedtime Stories

Bedtime stories help transition children into a relaxed state. A gentle story creates comfort and emotional security.

Create a Comfortable Sleep Environment

A quiet, dark, and comfortable room helps children fall asleep faster. Keep the space calm and free from distractions.

Be Patient and Consistent

Sleep habits take time to build. Stay consistent and allow your child to adjust gradually.
